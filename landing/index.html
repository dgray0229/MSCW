The Science of Constraint-Driven Planning

Studies in cognitive psychology reveal that human beings can only maintain active focus on **3 to 4 major tasks** per day. By giving tasks a weight based on complexity (the Fibonacci sequence) and setting a absolute limit of **8 points**, FocusMust aligns with your natural brain constraints.

An 8-point limit represents a standardized, fully productive 8-hour workday. In Fibonacci sizing, an 8-point task is a full-day epic, whereas 1-point and 2-point tasks represent brief routines. Limiting to 8 points stops the urge of over-scheduling.

Incomplete tasks linger in our subconscious, causing anxiety and background fatigue. By parking non-negotiable "Won't-Haves" safely in the sprint backlog, FocusMust signals to your brain that they are planned for later, clearing immediate mental RAM.

When you overload a day, the AI Guardian intervenes. Instead of letting you fail, it acts as a digital advisor, recommending you delegate lesser Could-Haves to clear space for core priorities.
